# NorthWing Labs Website Sitemap

This document defines the approved Version 1 sitemap for the NorthWing Labs website.

The goal is to keep the site focused, credible, and conversion-oriented without creating thin or artificial pages. The structure should clearly explain what NorthWing Labs does, what business problems it solves, how engagements work, and how a prospective customer can start a conversation.

## 3. Home

### Purpose

Explain what NorthWing Labs does, who it helps, what problems it solves, and why the visitor should continue exploring or start a conversation.

### Primary Conversion Goal

**Start a Conversation**

### Secondary Conversion Goal

**Explore Our Services**

### Recommended Homepage Structure

```text
Home
├── Header / Navigation
├── Hero
├── Problem Recognition
├── Services Overview
├── Business Outcomes
├── How We Work
├── Featured Project / Demonstration
├── Why NorthWing Labs
├── Results / Value Metrics
├── Final CTA
└── Footer
```

### Questions the Homepage Must Answer

- What does NorthWing Labs do?
- Who is NorthWing Labs for?
- What business problems can NorthWing Labs solve?
- What can NorthWing Labs build?
- What does working together look like?
- Why should a prospective customer trust NorthWing Labs?
- What should the visitor do next?

### Purpose

Explain what NorthWing Labs can build.

### Version 1 Structure

Use one Services page with five major service sections.

```text
Services
├── Custom Software
├── AI Tools
├── Business Automation
├── Mobile Apps
└── SaaS Development
```

### Individual Service Pages

Dedicated individual service pages are approved (superseding the
earlier "Pages to Delay" guidance below). Approved individual service
pages:

- Custom Software (`/services/custom-software` — GitHub issue #18)
- AI Tools (`/services/ai-tools` — GitHub issue #20)
- Business Automation (`/services/business-automation` — GitHub issue #22)

### Standard Section Structure

Each service section should include:

- What the service is
- Common business problems it solves
- Example use cases
- Expected business outcomes
- CTA to discuss a project

### Service Positioning Principle

Lead with the business problem and outcome before technical implementation details.

## 5. Solutions

### Purpose

Explain the business problems NorthWing Labs can solve.

This page should be organized around customer pain points, not technical service categories.

```text
Solutions
├── Save Time and Reduce Manual Work
├── Improve Customer Response
├── Connect Disconnected Systems
├── Improve Business Visibility
├── Modernize Spreadsheet Processes
└── Build New Digital Products
```

### Positioning Principle

Many small-business owners know the problem they have but do not know which technical solution they need.

The Solutions page should help visitors recognize their problem and understand that NorthWing Labs can design the appropriate technology response.

### Version 1 Structure

```text
Work
├── Internal Products
├── Demonstrations
└── Case Studies
```

### Initial Content Opportunities

Potential early examples may include:

- NorthWing Labs Website Project
- Woodworkers Pricing & Profit Toolkit
- Business Workflow Demonstrations
- Future Client Projects

### Transparency Rule

Clearly identify whether an item is:

- A client project
- An internal NorthWing Labs product
- A demonstration
- A concept

Do not create fictional client case studies.

## 7. Process

### Purpose

Reduce uncertainty and explain how a NorthWing Labs engagement works.

```text
Process
├── 1. Discover
├── 2. Define
├── 3. Design
├── 4. Build
├── 5. Test
├── 6. Launch
└── 7. Improve
```

### Process Definitions

#### Discover

Understand the business, workflow, problem, users, current tools, and desired outcome.

#### Define

Clarify requirements, priorities, scope, constraints, and success criteria.

#### Design

Plan the user experience, workflows, screens, system behavior, and technical direction.

#### Build

Develop the solution in controlled, reviewable phases.

#### Test

Validate functionality, usability, responsiveness, accessibility, and reliability.

#### Launch

Deploy the solution and confirm production readiness.

#### Improve

Measure results, gather feedback, and identify opportunities for refinement.

## 8. About

### Purpose

Establish credibility, explain why NorthWing Labs exists, and communicate the company’s operating philosophy.

### Recommended Structure

```text
About
├── Why NorthWing Labs Exists
├── Mission
├── Who We Help
├── Our Approach
├── What We Believe
└── Founder Story
```

### Positioning Principle

NorthWing Labs should not pretend to be a large agency.

The page should emphasize:

- Direct access
- Clear communication
- Practical problem solving
- Real business understanding
- Responsible technology implementation
- Long-term partnership mindset

## 9. Contact

### Purpose

Convert qualified visitors into project conversations.

Recommended page framing:

**Tell Us What You’re Trying to Improve**

### Recommended Structure

```text
Contact
├── Brief Introduction
├── Project Type
├── Business Problem
├── Desired Outcome
├── Current Tools / Process
├── Timeline
├── Budget Range
└── Contact Information
```

### Contact Principle

The form should feel like the beginning of a useful discovery conversation, not a generic contact form.

## 11. Pages to Delay

Do not build these in Version 1 unless there is a clear business need and enough meaningful content:

- Blog
- Resources
- Careers
- Industry-specific pages
- Large case-study library
- Pricing page

Individual service pages were originally delayed but are now approved
and being built — see "Individual Service Pages" in section 4.

These can be added later when content depth, SEO opportunity, or sales needs justify them.

## 12. Version 1 Strategy

The Version 1 website should be:

- Focused
- Credible
- Easy to navigate
- Conversion-oriented
- Honest about company maturity
- Strong enough to support high-value conversations
- Flexible enough to expand later

```text
Process
```
